# Transportation Engineering Lab - Question Bank 


## 1. California Bearing Ratio (CBR) Test

### Basic Viva Questions

**Q1. What is CBR?**
CBR is the ratio of load required to penetrate soil to standard load, expressed in percentage.

**Q2. Why is CBR test important?**
It is used to determine subgrade strength for flexible pavement design.

**Q3. What is standard penetration in CBR test?**
2.5 mm and 5.0 mm penetration.

**Q4. Which value of CBR is generally taken for design?**
Higher of 2.5 mm and 5 mm penetration values.

### Conceptual Questions

**Q5. Why is CBR still widely used in India?**
Because it is simple, economical, and well-correlated with field performance.

**Q6. Can two soils with same CBR behave differently?**
Yes, due to differences in drainage, plasticity, and moisture sensitivity.

### Typical Values

* Poor subgrade: 2% – 5%
* Fair subgrade: 5% – 10%
* Good subgrade: 10% – 20%
* Very good subgrade: > 20%

### Code Reference

* IS 2720 (Part 16)
* IRC:37 (Flexible Pavement Design)

## 2. Aggregate Crushing Value (ACV)

### Viva Questions

**Q1. What does ACV indicate?**
Resistance of aggregates to compressive loading.

**Q2. Why is crushing strength important in pavements?**
Because aggregates directly carry wheel loads in base layers.

### Conceptual Questions

**Q3. Why are weaker aggregates not used in base course?**
They break under repeated traffic loading leading to rutting.

**Q4. What is the effect of angular aggregates on ACV?**
Angular aggregates generally show better interlocking and lower failure.

### Typical Values

* High quality pavement: < 30%
* Base course: < 45%
* Sub-base: < 50%

### IS Code

* IS 2386 (Part 4)

---

## 3. Aggregate Impact Value (AIV)

### Viva Questions

**Q1. What is AIV?**
It measures toughness of aggregates under sudden impact load.

### Conceptual Questions

**Q2. Why is impact test required if crushing test already exists?**
Because traffic loads are dynamic and not purely static.

**Q3. Which is more severe: ACV or AIV?**
AIV represents more severe field-like condition.

### Typical Values

* Wearing course: < 20%
* Base course: < 30%

## 4. Los Angeles Abrasion Test

### Viva Questions

**Q1. What does abrasion represent?**
Wear and tear due to traffic friction.

### Conceptual Questions

**Q2. Why is abrasion critical for surface course?**
Because top layer is directly exposed to tire action.

### Typical Values

* Surface course: < 30%
* Base course: < 40–50%

---

## 5. Flakiness & Elongation Index

### Viva Questions

**Q1. What are flaky aggregates?**
Aggregates with thickness less than 0.6 of mean size.

**Q2. What are elongated aggregates?**
Aggregates with length greater than 1.8 times mean size.

### Conceptual Questions

**Q3. Why are flaky aggregates harmful?**
They reduce interlocking and increase voids.

**Q4. Why is shape important in highways?**
Better shape → better stability and load transfer.

## 6. Penetration Test (Bitumen)

### Viva Questions

**Q1. What does penetration value indicate?**
Hardness/consistency of bitumen.

### Conceptual Questions

**Q2. Why is bitumen grade important?**
It ensures performance under climatic conditions.

**Q3. What happens if very soft bitumen is used in hot regions?**
Bleeding and rutting occur.

### Typical Values

* 30/40 (hard grade, hot regions)
* 60/70 (standard paving grade in India)
* 80/100 (cold regions)

### IS Code

* IS 1203

---

## 7. Ductility Test

### Viva Questions

**Q1. What is ductility?**
Ability of bitumen to stretch without breaking.

### Conceptual Questions

**Q2. Why is ductility important in pavements?**
It prevents cracking under temperature variations.

### Typical Value

* Minimum: 75 cm (paving grade bitumen)

## 8. Softening Point Test

### Viva Questions

**Q1. What does softening point indicate?**
Temperature at which bitumen loses stiffness.

### Conceptual Questions

**Q2. Why is softening point important in India?**
High temperature regions require high softening point bitumen.

### Typical Range

* 35°C – 70°C depending on grade

---

## 9. Flash and Fire Point

### Viva Questions

**Q1. Difference between flash and fire point?**
Flash: momentary ignition
Fire: continuous burning

### Conceptual Questions

**Q2. Why is this test essential in labs?**
For safety during heating and mixing of bitumen.

### Typical Values

* Flash point: > 220°C

## 10. Bitumen Extraction Test

### Viva Questions

**Q1. What does this test determine?**
Bitumen content in asphalt mix.

### Conceptual Questions

**Q2. What happens if bitumen content is high?**
Bleeding and reduced skid resistance.

**Q3. What happens if bitumen content is low?**
Ravelling and premature failure.

## 11. Benkelman Beam Test

### Viva Questions

**Q1. What does this test measure?**
Pavement deflection under load.

### Conceptual Questions

**Q2. Why is deflection important?**
It indicates structural capacity of pavement.

**Q3. What does high deflection mean?**
Weak pavement or subgrade failure.

### Typical Values

* Good pavement: < 1 mm
* Weak pavement: > 2 mm

### IRC Reference

* IRC:81 (Overlay design)

---

## 12. High-Level Examiner Questions (Very Important)

**Q1. Why do pavements fail even if materials pass all lab tests?**
Due to poor drainage, compaction, workmanship, and environmental effects.

**Q2. Why is CBR empirical but still widely used?**
Because it correlates well with field performance and is simple.

**Q3. Why is IRC preferred over IS for highways?**
IRC provides design-oriented guidelines specific to pavements.

**Q4. Can aggregate shape affect pavement life?**
Yes, poor shape leads to poor interlocking and early failure.

**Q5. What is more critical: strength or durability of pavement?**
Durability, because long-term performance depends on it.
