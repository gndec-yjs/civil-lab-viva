# Concrete Technology Laboratory - Question Bank

# 1. CEMENT TESTS

## 1.1 Fineness Test

**IS Code:** IS 4031 (Part 2)

**Principle:**
Finer cement has larger surface area, which increases hydration rate and early strength.

**Typical Value:**
225–350 m²/kg (Blaine)

**Q1. What is fineness of cement?**
It is the measure of particle size of cement.

**Q2. Why is fineness important?**
It increases rate of hydration and early strength.

**Q3. Why is very fine cement not always preferred?**
It causes high heat of hydration and shrinkage cracks.

**Examiner WHY Question:**
Why does fineness increase strength?
Because larger surface area allows faster chemical reaction with water.

---

## 1.2 Standard Consistency

**IS Code:** IS 4031 (Part 4)

**Principle:**
It determines water required to initiate hydration reaction in cement paste.

**Typical Range:** 26%–33%

**Q:** What is standard consistency?
Water required for 33–35 mm Vicat penetration.

**WHY:**
Why is consistency test required before other tests?
Because all cement tests depend on water content.

---

## 1.3 Setting Time

**IS Code:** IS 4031 (Part 5)

**Principle:**
Hydration process causes cement to lose plasticity and gain strength.

**Typical Values:**

* Initial ≥ 30 min
* Final ≤ 10 hours

**Q:** Why is initial setting time important?
It provides workable time for mixing, transporting, and placing concrete.

**Tricky Question:**
Why does cement set faster in hot weather?
Due to accelerated hydration reaction.

---

## 1.4 Soundness Test

**IS Code:** IS 4031 (Part 3)

**Principle:**
Checks volume stability of cement after hydration.

**Typical Expansion:** ≤ 10 mm

**WHY:**
Why is soundness important?
To prevent cracks due to delayed expansion of lime or magnesia.

---

## 1.5 Compressive Strength of Cement

**IS Code:** IS 4031 (Part 6)

**Principle:**
Measures resistance of cement mortar cube to compressive load.

# 2. AGGREGATE TESTS

## 2.1 Aggregate Crushing Value (ACV)

**IS Code:** IS 2386 (Part 4)

**Principle:**
Resistance of aggregate to gradually applied compressive load.

**Typical Values:**

* Wearing course: < 30%
* Base course: < 45%

**WHY:**
Why are aggregates important in load transfer?
Because they form skeleton of concrete.

---

## 2.2 Aggregate Impact Value (AIV)

**Principle:**
Measures resistance to sudden impact or shock load.

**Typical Values:**

* High quality: < 20%
* Base course: < 30%

**WHY:**
Why is AIV more severe than ACV?
Because traffic loads are dynamic and impact-based.

---

## 2.3 Los Angeles Abrasion Test

**Principle:**
Measures wear resistance due to friction and abrasion.

**Typical Values:**

* Surface course: < 30%
* Sub-base: < 50%

---

## 2.4 Shape Tests (Flakiness & Elongation)

**Principle:**
Checks particle geometry and interlocking ability.

* Flaky: thickness < 0.6 × size
* Elongated: length > 1.8 × size

**WHY:**
Why are flaky aggregates harmful?
They reduce interlocking and increase voids.

---

## 2.5 Specific Gravity & Water Absorption

**IS Code:** IS 2386 (Part 3)

**Typical Values:**

* SG: 2.6–2.8
* Water absorption: < 2%

**WHY:**
Higher water absorption → weaker aggregates.

---

# 3. WORKABILITY TESTS

## 3.1 Slump Test

**IS Code:** IS 1199

**Principle:**
Measures deformation of concrete under its own weight.

**Types:**

* True slump
* Shear slump
* Collapse slump

**Typical Values:**

* Pavements: 20–40 mm
* RCC: 50–100 mm
* Pumped concrete: 100–150 mm

**WHY:**
Why is slump not suitable for very dry concrete?
Because it gives zero or unreliable readings.

---

## 3.2 Compaction Factor Test

**Principle:**
Ratio of partially compacted to fully compacted concrete weight.

**Typical Values:**

* Dry: 0.7–0.8
* Medium: 0.8–0.9
* High: 0.9–0.95

---

## 3.3 Vee-Bee Test

**Principle:**
Measures time required for concrete to remould under vibration.

Used for stiff concrete (zero slump).

---

# 4. STRENGTH OF CONCRETE

## 4.1 Compressive Strength Test

**IS Code:** IS 516
Cube: 150 mm

**Principle:**
Measures ultimate load resistance before failure.

**Grades:**

* M20 = 20 MPa
* M25 = 25 MPa
* M30 = 30 MPa

**WHY:**
Why 28 days strength is standard?
Because hydration stabilizes after 28 days.

---

## 4.2 Split Tensile Strength

**IS Code:** IS 5816
Concrete is weak in tension; this test evaluates tensile behavior indirectly.

---

## 4.3 Flexural Strength

Measures bending resistance (important for pavements).

---

# 5. NON-DESTRUCTIVE TESTING (NDT)

## 5.1 Rebound Hammer Test

**IS Code:** IS 13311 (Part 2)

**Principle:**
Surface hardness is correlated with compressive strength.

**WHY:**
Why is it not fully reliable?
Because surface condition affects readings.

---

## 5.2 UPV Test

**IS Code:** IS 13311 (Part 1)

**Principle:**
Velocity of ultrasonic waves indicates concrete quality.

**Ranges:**

* > 4.5 km/s → Excellent
* 3.5–4.5 km/s → Good
* < 3.0 km/s → Poor

---

# 6. MIX DESIGN

**IS Code:** IS 10262

**Principle:**
Optimized proportioning of concrete ingredients for desired strength and durability.

**w/c ratio:** 0.4–0.6

**WHY:**
Why is low w/c ratio important?
It reduces porosity and increases strength.

# 7. FIELD & PRACTICAL QUESTIONS

## Cement Field Knowledge

**Q1. Weight of cement bag?** 50 kg
**Q2. Volume of cement bag?** 0.0347 m³
**Q3. Shelf life?** 3 months
**Q4. Storage method?** Dry, elevated platform, moisture-free

---

## Field Tests of Cement

* Grey colour with slight green shade
* No hard lumps
* Should feel cool on hand
* Should sink after floating briefly in water

---

## Concrete Field Defects

**Bleeding:** water rises to surface
**Segregation:** separation of ingredients
**Honeycombing:** voids due to poor compaction

---

## Field Failure Questions

**Q:** Why does concrete crack after curing?
Due to shrinkage, thermal stress, or poor curing.

**Q:** Why do pavements fail even with good aggregates?
Due to poor drainage, compaction, or subgrade failure.

---

## Workability Concept

**Q:** Is higher slump always better?
No, it may reduce strength due to excess water.

---

# 8. HIGH LEVEL EXAMINER QUESTIONS

**Q1. Strength vs durability difference?**
Strength = load capacity
Durability = resistance to environment

**Q2. Why NDT is preferred?**
Non-destructive and field-safe evaluation.

**Q3. Why IS codes are essential?**
Standardization and reliability of testing.

**Q4. Why field strength differs from lab strength?**
Due to curing, compaction, and workmanship variations.
