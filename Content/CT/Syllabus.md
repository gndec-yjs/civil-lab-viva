# Concrete Technology Laboratory - Syllabus

## Course Information

**Course Name:** Concrete Technology Laboratory
**Program:** B.Tech Civil Engineering

## Course Objectives

The Concrete Technology Laboratory is designed to:

* Provide practical understanding of properties of cement, aggregates, and concrete
* Enable students to perform standard tests as per IS codes
* Understand behavior of concrete in fresh and hardened states
* Develop skills in mix design and quality control of concrete
* Introduce Non-Destructive Testing (NDT) methods for assessment of concrete structures

## Learning Outcomes

After completion of this laboratory, students will be able to:

1. Determine engineering properties of cement, aggregates, and concrete
2. Conduct standard tests as per relevant IS codes
3. Evaluate workability of fresh concrete using different methods
4. Determine compressive, tensile, and flexural strength of concrete
5. Perform basic non-destructive testing on concrete specimens
6. Interpret test results for quality control and construction applications

## Relevant IS Codes

* IS 269 / IS 8112 / IS 12269 – Specifications for cement
* IS 4031 – Methods of testing cement
* IS 2386 – Methods of testing aggregates
* IS 10262 – Concrete mix design
* IS 456 – Plain and reinforced concrete code
* IS 516 – Compressive strength of concrete
* IS 1199 – Workability tests
* IS 516 – Non-destructive testing of concrete

## Viva Preparation Guidance

Students should be able to answer:

* Purpose of each test
* Principle behind each experiment
* Interpretation of results
* Effect of water-cement ratio on strength
* Difference between various workability tests
* Why NDT is used instead of destructive testing in field conditions
